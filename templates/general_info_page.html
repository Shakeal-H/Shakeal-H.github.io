General Pokémon Information

What are Pokémon?

Pokémon are creatures of all shapes and sizes who live in the wild or alongside their human partners (called “Trainers”). During their adventures, Pokémon grow and become more experienced and even, on occasion, evolve into stronger Pokémon. Hundreds of known Pokémon inhabit the Pokémon universe, with untold numbers waiting to be discovered! There are currently 905 Pokémon in the National Pokedex.

How many Pokémon types are there?

According to Bulbapedia there are 18 Unique Pokémon types. Types are properties applied to Pokémon and their moves, which affect the power of moves in battles. As of Generation VI, there are 18 types, as listed to the right. Most of these were introduced during Generation I, but the Dark and Steel types were introduced in Generation II, and the Fairy type was introduced in Generation VI. A unique '???' type also existed from Generations II through IV. During Generation I, types were occasionally referred to as elements. The types are based on the concept of classical elements in popular culture.

What parameters can be searched with this tool?

In addition to type effectiveness mentioned above there are a variety of categorical and quantitative variables that can be used to filter the 890 Pokémon within our tool.

List of Categorical Attributes

  • Type: Types refer to different elemental properties associated with the Pokémon, their eggs and their moves. With primary being the main type and secondary being an additional type.
  • Generation: Generation refers to the Pokémon game series, there are currently 8 generations.
  • Species: A brief descriptor for Pokémon found in the Pokédex that may be shared by different Pokémon.
  • Abilities: An Ability is a game mechanic introduced in Generation III that provides a passive effect in battle or in the overworld. A pokemon can have up to 2 abilities and one hidden ability.
  • Growth Rate: The amount of experience a pokemon needs to level up is determined by its growth rate group.

List of Base Stats Attributes

  • Attack: A stat which determines the power of a physical move.
  • Defense: A stat which partly determines how much damage a Pokémon receives when it is hit with a physical move.
  • Special Attack: A stat which partly determines the how much damage a Pokemon deals on a special move.
  • Special Defense: A stat partly determines how much damage a Pokémon receives when it is hit with a special move.
  • HP: The HP short for Hit Point determines how much damage a Pokémon can receive before fainting.
  • Speed: The Speed stat determines the order of Pokémon that can act in battle.

List of Quantitative Attributes

  • Height: How tall the pokemon is in meters.
  • Weight: The weight of the Pokemon in kilograms.
  • Catch Rate: When a Poké Ball is thrown at a wild Pokémon, the game uses that Pokémon's catch rate in a formula
    to determine the chances of catching that Pokémon.
  • Experience: The amount of experience an individual Pokémon has is an indication of how much it has battled.
  • Base Friendship: The value to which its friendship is set when the Pokémon is caught, received from an event or NPC,
    or received from a trade with another player.
  • Percentage Male: The percentage of male Pokemon in a species.
